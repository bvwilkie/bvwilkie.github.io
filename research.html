Background & Experience

In 2010, I completed a Bachelor of Arts at Monash University with majors in History and Philosophy. Prior to this, I studied performance and musicology at the Sir Zelman Cowen School of Music. I completed a PhD in Australian history at Monash University in 2014. Between 2014 and 2017, I was Lecturer in Australian Studies at Deakin University, where I am currently a Honorary Fellow in the School of Humanities & Social Sciences. Prior to this, I was a teaching associate at Monash University and a research assistant with the CRC for Water Sensitive Cities. Throughout this time, I have also written extensively for a variety of media outlets and popular publications on history, culture, and politics in Australia.

Research Projects

The Scots in Australia

Since 2009, I have researched and written widely on the historical relationships between Scotland, Australia, and the British Empire. I have explored Scottish identity and culture in Australia, Scottish-Indigenous relations, visual and material culture, the relationship between Scots and the Australian environment and landscape, and the role of space and place in the migrant experience. My book, The Scots in Australia 1788-1938, was published by Boydell Press and the Scottish Historical Review Trust in November 2017.

Grampians-Gariwerd

I am currently writing an environmental, social, and cultural history of the Grampians-Gariwerd national park in the Western District of Victoria. I am interested in its geological and ecological development, its Indigenous and European histories, its more recent story as a national park, including Indigenous planning and land management, and conservation issues into the future. My book, The Grampians: History and Nature, is to be released by CSIRO Publishing in 2019.

Environment, Agriculture, and War

Since late-2015, I have been exploring the many intersections of Australian environmental, agricultural, and military history. I am currently investigating environmental changes at military training areas and the broader evolution of sustainable land management practices in the Australian defence forces, and I am also exploring the relationships between war and agricultural production in Australia during the twentieth century.
