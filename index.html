About Me

I am an Australian historian, writer, and teacher. I am the author of Gariwerd: An Environmental History of the Grampians and The Scots in Australia: 1788-1938. I am also a freelance writer and contribute to a range of publications on diverse subjects including classical music, religion, and history.

My Research

Empire

Scotland and the British Empire

My research on the histories of Scotland and the British Empire has focused on Scottish migration, settlement, culture, businesses, and politics in Australasia and Africa. I am the author of The Scots in Australia: 1788-1938 and in 2019 was a Visiting Research Fellow at the University of Glasgow. I am currently an Associate of the Centre for Irish and Scottish Studies at the University of Otago.

Environment

Victorian environmental history

My second book, Gariwerd: An Environmental History of the Grampians, is an environmental and social history of the Gariwerd mountain ranges and Grampians National Park in the Western District of Victoria. I have also published on the history of land management and conservation in Australia's defence forces. I am currently researching the environmental, social, and economic histories of the Newer Volcanics Province of south-east Australia.

Enterprise

The corporate elite in 20th century Australia

As a Research Assistant in the Macquarie Business School's Department of Management, I am contributing to a project investigating interlocking directorates, where company directors sit on more than one board, in the top 100 Australian financial and industrial firms throughout the twentieth century.
