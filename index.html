About

I am an Australian historian, writer, and academic. My research spans Australian and Scottish imperial and colonial history, business history, and environmental history. I am currently a research assistant at Macquarie Business School and an Associate of the Centre for Irish and Scottish Studies at the University of Otago. Additionally, I am a regular contributor to the classical music publication, Limelight Magazine. In 2019 I am a Visiting Research Fellow at the University of Glasgow.

Background & Experience

In 2010, I completed a Bachelor of Arts at Monash University with majors in History and Philosophy. Prior to this, I studied performance and musicology at the Sir Zelman Cowen School of Music, and I am currently a regular writer for the classical music magazine, Limelight. As a historian I have been particularly interested in Scottish social networks, business connections, migration, and culture abroad, and my 2014 PhD thesis at Monash University explored the historical relationships between Scotland, Australia, and the British Empire. In 2014, I was elected as a Fellow of Society of Antiquaries of Scotland. My first book, The Scots in Australia 1788-1938, was published by the Scottish Historical Review Trust and Boydell Press in 2017.

In 2019, I was a Visiting Research Fellow at the University of Glasgow, where I investigated the historical connections between Scotland and Africa. I am currently a Research Assistant at Macquarie Business School and an Associate of the Centre for Irish and Scottish Studies at the University of Otago. Prior to this, between 2013 and 2017, I was Lecturer in Australian Studies at Deakin University. I have also been a teaching associate at Monash University, a research assistant with the CRC for Water Sensitive Cities, and an editorial assistant with the Australian Journal of Political Science.

My current ongoing research projects include the history of Scottish contributions to the development of colonial economies, as well as research in environmental history; my second book, Gariwerd: An Environmental History of the Grampians, will be released by CSIRO Publishing in 2020.
