About

I am an Australian historian, writer, and academic. My research focuses on histories of Australia's society and environment. My first book is The Scots in Australia 1788-1938, and I am currently writing The Grampians: History & Nature. In addition to academic and popular histories, I write on Australian arts, culture, and politics for a variety of publications.

Background & Experience

In 2010, I completed a Bachelor of Arts at Monash University with majors in History and Philosophy. Prior to this, I studied performance and musicology at the Sir Zelman Cowen School of Music. I completed a PhD in Australian history at Monash University in 2014. Between 2014 and 2017, I was Lecturer in Australian Studies at Deakin University. I am currently an associate of the Centre for Irish and Scottish Studies at the University of Otago. Prior to this, I have been a teaching associate at Monash University, a research assistant with the CRC for Water Sensitive Cities, and an editorial assistant with the Australian Journal of Political Science. I am also a regular contributor to the classical music publication, Limelight Magazine
