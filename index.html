About

I am an Australian historian, writer, and academic. My scholarly research spans imperial and colonial history, economic and business history, and environmental history. I am a Visiting Research Fellow at the University of Glasgow, a Research Assistant in the Faculty of Business & Economics at Macquarie University, and an Associate of the Centre for Irish and Scottish Studies at the University of Otago. Background & Experience

In 2010, I completed a Bachelor of Arts at Monash University with majors in History and Philosophy. Prior to this, I studied performance and musicology at the Sir Zelman Cowen School of Music. I completed a PhD in Australian history at Monash University in 2014. In the same year I was nominated as a Fellow of the Society of Antiquaries of Scotland. I am currently an Associate of the Centre for Irish and Scottish Studies at the University of Otago, and in 2019 I am a Visiting Research Fellow at the University of Glasgow. Prior to this, between 2014 and 2017, I was Lecturer in Australian Studies at Deakin University. I have also been a teaching associate at Monash University, a research assistant with the CRC for Water Sensitive Cities, and an editorial assistant with the Australian Journal of Political Science. Additionally, I am a regular contributor to the classical music publication, Limelight Magazine
