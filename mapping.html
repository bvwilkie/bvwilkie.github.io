Mapping Frontier Violence

I am currently compiling a range of primary and secondary historical sources and building a dataset that will form the basis of an interactive map of Indigenous-European frontier conflict in nineteenth-century Australia. The first stage of the project will focus on Western Victoria. The map below is an early example, and displays recorded killings and massacres of Dhauwurd wurrung, or Gunditjmara, people in south-west Victoria, Watha wurrung in the Geelong region, and Wergaia in the Wimmera area in north-west Victoria.
